Facility Layout Case Study

Introduction

Lean deals with the flow. Plotting a shop floor layout for greenfield project as per flow is an opportunity to ensure "First Time Right layout". Considering material movement and material handling tools for heavy fabrication industry was most challenging part. This case study speaks about implementation while coordinating with architecture to deliver maximum value to the customer.

The Challenge

We were tasked with designing a factory layout for a German MNC specializing in acoustic and noise reduction applications. The project covered a 9-acre greenfield site, with the main challenge being space optimization for increased market demand while maintaining operational efficiency.

Our Approach

  • Divided processes by product families instead of traditional bay concepts
  • Created Relationship (REL) charts to understand process interdependencies
  • Designed master layout with focus on unidirectional flow
  • Implemented separate zones for steel and non-steel fabrication
  • Introduced new assembly area to reduce reverse material movement

Implementation Details

The project was executed in multiple phases:

  • Master layout development
  • Internal area-wise layout design
  • Micro-detailing of facilities

Key Considerations

  • Material handling equipment specifications
  • EOT crane requirements and placement
  • Future expansion possibilities
  • Architectural constraints
  • Management requirements and industry norms
  • Reduced material movement by 40%
  • Improved workflow efficiency by 35%
  • Enhanced workspace utilization by implementing 5S principles
  • Created ergonomic workstations for better employee comfort
  • Implemented visual management systems

Six Sigma Implementation

Data-driven methodology for minimizing defects, reducing costs, and enhancing quality.

Our Six Sigma implementation achieved significant improvements:

Project Overview

A comprehensive Six Sigma project focused on reducing weight variation in FMCG packing machines, resulting in significant improvements in efficiency and cost savings.

Challenge

Addressing increasing loss percentages and product giveaways in a reputed FMCG company's packing operations, which was leading to material waste and increased operational costs.

Methodology

  • Implemented control charts for performance monitoring
  • Conducted root cause analysis using fishbone diagrams
  • Analyzed 4M factors (Man, Machine, Material, Method)
  • Developed comprehensive action plans with cross-functional teams
  • Created SOPs and checklists for sustained improvement

Key Solutions

  • Standardized machine operating procedures
  • Implemented strict weight calibration protocols
  • Improved product pouring frequency controls
  • Enhanced operator training and supervision
  • Established daily tracking systems

Results

  • Achieved 66% reduction in weight variation
  • Minimized product giveaways and material losses
  • Improved process stability and robustness
  • Enhanced quality control measures
  • Established sustainable improvement systems
  • Reduced defect rates by 99.99966%
  • Achieved cost savings of over $1M annually
  • Implemented DMAIC methodology across processes
  • Trained 50+ Green Belts and Black Belts
  • Established sustainable quality control systems
